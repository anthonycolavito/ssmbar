About this dashboard. Benefits are computed at claim age 65 under current law, using SSA's 2025 Trustees Report intermediate assumptions. Real values are expressed in 2026 dollars using the GDP price index. Period unisex life expectancy at age 65 is the simple average of male and female period LE-at-65 from the Trustees Report, not a weighted population average. Earnings profiles follow SSA scaled-earner conventions (very-low, low, medium, high, max). A worker with custom dollar earnings — e.g., $50,000 — is not directly modeled, but their benefit can be approximated by interpolating between adjacent scaled types. Spousal benefits are modeled at the household level: the worker's own retired-worker benefit, the worker's spousal benefit on the partner's record, and the partner's spousal benefit on the worker's record. Both spouses are assumed to share the worker's birth year and claim age. Replacement rates are first-year benefit ratios, not lifetime averages.
